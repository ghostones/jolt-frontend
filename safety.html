Safety on JOLT

Random video chat only works when people feel comfortable. JOLT is built with a safety-first mindset — not as an afterthought, but as a foundation.

While no online platform can guarantee perfection, JOLT is designed to reduce risk, discourage abuse, and respond quickly when rules are broken.

Our Community Standards

Everyone using JOLT is expected to follow a simple set of rules:

  • No nudity or sexually explicit behavior
  • No harassment, hate speech, or threats
  • No abusive, aggressive, or intimidating conduct
  • No attempts to exploit or target minors
  • No illegal activity of any kind

These rules exist to protect users and keep conversations comfortable for everyone.

How Reporting Works

If a conversation feels inappropriate or unsafe, users can report it immediately.

Reporting on JOLT is designed to be easy to access, fast to submit, and taken seriously to help identify harmful behavior.

How Moderation Is Enforced

JOLT focuses on prevention first, not just reaction.

  • Clear rules shown before interaction
  • Active moderation signals
  • Systems designed to discourage repeat abuse

What Happens When Rules Are Broken

Users who violate community standards may face:

  • Immediate disconnection
  • Temporary limitations
  • Permanent bans for serious or repeated violations

Enforcement decisions are made to protect the broader community, not to maximize usage metrics.

What JOLT Does Not Allow

To be clear, JOLT is not:

  • An adult content platform
  • A shock-based random chat site
  • A place for explicit or exploitative behavior

User Responsibility & Expectations

Safety is a shared responsibility. Users are encouraged to follow the rules, use reporting tools when needed, and leave conversations that feel uncomfortable.

If a platform ever stops feeling safe, the right choice is to disengage.

JOLT is built for people who want a random video chat experience that feels human, calm, and respectful.
